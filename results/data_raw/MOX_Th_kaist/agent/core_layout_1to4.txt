Format : ASCII, one row per line.  Coordinates are (col, row) with col

----------------------------------------------------
   The 1/4-core figure shows a 4-row x 4-col block in the lower-left
   quadrant of the full core.  The reflective boundary is on the
   LEFT and BOTTOM of the figure (the two symmetry axes of the full
   core pass through these edges); the reflector and vacuum
   boundary are on the TOP and RIGHT.

   Cells (read top to bottom in the PDF = outermost to innermost
   in the full core):

      row 0 (2 cells, outermost, closest to reflector)
          col 2 : UOX-1
          col 3 : UOX-1

      row 1 (3 cells)
          col 1 : MOX-1
          col 2 : UOX-2 (CR)
          col 3 : UOX-1

      row 2 (4 cells)
          col 0 : UOX-2 (CR)
          col 1 : MOX-1 (BA8)
          col 2 : UOX-2 (CR)

      row 3 (4 cells, innermost, corner of full core)

   Total: 5 x UOX-1,  4 x UOX-2(CR),  2 x MOX-1,  1 x MOX-1(BA8),  1 x UOX-2(BA16)

ASSEMBLY-TYPE NAMES
-------------------
     asy_uox1        :  Standard UOX-1 (no BA, no CR); 24 water-filled
                        guide tubes + 1 instrument tube
     asy_uox2        :  Standard UOX-2 (no BA, no CR); 24 water-filled
                        guide tubes + 1 instrument tube
     asy_uox2gd      :  UOX-2 with 16 Gd2O3 BA rods (BA16)
     asy_mox1z       :  MOX-1 with 3 Pu-enrichment zones (no BA, no CR)
     asy_mox1z_ba8   :  MOX-1 with 3 zones and 8 Gd2O3 BA rods (BA8)

   "CR" assemblies (UOX-2(CR) in the figure) use the same
   asy_uox2 lattice as plain UOX-2.  The "(CR)" label only
   indicates that a control-rod cluster is allocated to that
   assembly.  In the ARO case the guide tubes are water-filled
   (same as asy_uox2).  In the ARI case the B4C rod is inserted
   into the guide tubes -- the asy_uox2_ari lattice (not shown
   here) should be used instead.

VOID / UNIVERSE 0
-----------------
     Cells with universe '0' (3 cells, outer corner of the 1/4) are
     outside the fueled region; they are filled with reflector
     water (cool material) in the global cell definition.
